The Curator Economy: Aggregating Users, Aggregating Liquidity (Part IV of IV)

Curator communities start looking more like micro economies when incentives become directly tied to follower engagement, growth, and commerce. Will today's ecosystem of waddling platform midlemen and clunky affiliate marketing links become outdated in a blink of an eye?

In Curator Economies, Curators will look to aggregate users much like how financial institutions or prototcols aggregate liquidity. Rewards for both Curator and their communities will tend towards growing together, rather than a huge chunk being handed over to platform middlemen.

However, there are many tools that need to be built in order to transition towards a fairer, more transparent Curator Economy. What would a Shopify for Curators look like? What else is missing in the "membership stack"? How do you measure follower reputation or contribution? What are some fair and transparent incentive systems? How might we transition a Curator community from a broadcast model (one-to-many) to a network model(many-to-many)?

If you're working on any of these ideas, or more, feel free to reach out and chat. I'm @taratan on Twitter and Telegram, or ttan [at] ideo [dot] com.
